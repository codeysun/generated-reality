TL;DR

Generated Reality is a concept that turns human-tracked data into an autoregressively generated video, enabling interactive human-centric experiences supporting dexterous hand-object interacctions.

Hover to visualize the (2D) hand pose conditioning. 3D hand and head pose conditioning not shown.


How it works

We track the user’s head and hand poses with a commercial VR headset. Hand motion is represented using the UmeTrack, which provides the wrist pose and 20 joint angles per hand. Our conditioning uses a hybrid 2D–3D scheme, combining a 2D image of hand skeleton and the 3D model hand-pose parameters. Features extracted from these modules are combined with the head pose features via token addition and fed into the DiT. The model then autoregressively generates new frames.


Ablations

Hand Motion Conditioning Strategies

We conduct a comprehensive ablation of hand-pose conditioning strategies and propose a 2D–3D hybrid approach that captures hand motion more reliably. Our method achieves the best hand pose accuracy among all baselines, especially when hands are partly occluded at the boundaries of the frame.

Joint Hand–Camera Conditioning

We further extend this framework to jointly condition on both hand and camera poses. Our joint-conditioning model correctly predicts user intent by reaching toward the correct cup on the left and placing the hand faithfully on the table, enabling accurate hand–object interaction.

The Generated Reality System

We develop our generated reality system by distilling the bidirectional model into an autoregressive variant that runs at interactive framerates on a VR headset. Using live tracked hand and head poses as conditioning signals, the system streams generated video directly to the device. In user studies, the hand-conditioned system enables significantly more accurate task completion and a much higher perceived level of control compared to the baseline.
